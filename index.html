About This Project

This quiz is not meant to discover a single “true” Spider-Man. Instead, it highlights how each film version emphasizes different traits, tones, and values while still drawing from the same larger character tradition. Raimi’s Spider-Man tends to foreground duty and sacrifice, Webb’s version stresses emotion and identity, and the MCU version emphasizes humor, mentorship, and growth. Those differences do not come from the source material alone, but from adaptation, collaboration, and interpretation across directors, actors, studios, and franchise expectations. In that sense, the scoring system models a broader academic idea about authorship: character identity is shaped through repeated reinterpretation rather than fixed originality. The result you receive is therefore less a final truth than a way of visualizing how creative choices influence the meaning of a familiar cultural figure.
